Evolution of Public Transport

What is public transport:

Pubilc Transport is a way for everyone to get from one place to another within the same locality or different one. Public trasnport includes all modes of transport from railways to waterways. It also poses as an economical resort to those without personal means of transport or to those who are unable to travel the whole journey.

Types of Public Transports:

  1. Railways
  2. Trams
  3. Buses
  4. Ferry
  5. etc.

What makes people choose Public Transport:

Infrastructure & Connectivity:

The public transport systems have vastly evolved around the world in the last few centuries thereby spreading their network to most parts of every cities. This makes all the parts of the cities accessible resulting in a good connectivity.

Economical

Public transports are generally very economical for those who cannot afford a personal vehicle or frequent taxi rides. Traversal in these is priced at a very nominal prices. Especially, for students this is the best option.

Safety

On comparison to the other modes of transport, public transport systems are comparitively safe. These are maintained frequently and the possibility of a casuality is relatively less.

The Evolution of Public Transport

The start of public transport dates back to the 1820s London and France with the Omnibus, a Horse-pulled car. Later, in 1855, permanent tram lines were setup. In the 1870s, cable cars were installed in San Francisco to aid the transportation in the hilly terrain. The 20th century started of with the production of the motorised double-deckered buses in London.


In the 1960s, bullet trains in Japans and Diesel Engines in the US were introduced. By the first decade of the 21st century, battery operated buses were in operation in China. In the future, the public transport is expected to turn towards more efficient, sustainable and environmental-friendly means.
